About the Conference

The Boston College 2nd PhD Conference brings together doctoral students in the field of economics. This conference is an opportunity to present and discuss doctoral research projects, with other students who share the same interest. In case of being selected, you will present your paper at the conference, and be assigned as discussant for the paper of another student. Please consider that students who present are expected to attend all the presentations.

We encourage students from all fields and programs to submitt a research paper. This conference offers a good opportunity to students in early stages of their PhD dissertation, as well as those close to completition.

We will have a Professor James Anderson as a keynote speaker, for all students to learn about his research trajectory, and vision about the current state of research in international trade policy. More information about Prof. James Anderson is provided below.

Paper Submission

Students can submit a full draft, or an extended abstract (500-1000 words). Priority, however, will be given to full drafts.

  • Include a title, your name, institutional affiliation, and contact information
  • Submit a PDF file via email to: BCEconGradConference@bc.edu
  • Use the subject line: "PhD Conference Submission - [Your Last Name]"

Submission deadline: March 1, 2026

Notification of acceptance: March 15, 2026

Keynote Speaker

Professor James E. Anderson

William B. Neenan, S.J. Millennium Professor of Economics, Boston College

James E. Anderson is internationally recognized for his foundational contributions to the theory of international trade and trade policy. His work on the economic foundations of the gravity model of trade has become a cornerstone of empirical research on how border frictions, tariffs, and institutional factors shape global commerce. His research spans theoretical innovation, influential quantifications of trade costs and agreements, and practical insights into trade barriers and economic integration. Professor Anderson is a Research Associate of the National Bureau of Economic Research and has received distinguished awards, including Boston College's Distinguished Senior Research Award.
